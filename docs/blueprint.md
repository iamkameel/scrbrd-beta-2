# **App Name**: SCRBRD Beta

## Core Features:

- Team Directory: Display a searchable list of cricket teams, with details such as school affiliation, division, and team suffix.
- Player Profiles: Present player profiles including career statistics (runs, wickets, catches).
- Match Fixtures: Display a schedule of upcoming and past matches, with details of the teams, date, time, and location.
- Match Results: Show match results, including scores, key statistics, and player performances.
- Awards and Accolades: List awards and accolades achieved by players and teams.
- Suggest Player Role: Use an LLM tool to suggest the Player's potential Playing Role
- Prematch Processes: Handle pre-match processes when a fixture is announced, including notifying coach and captain to pre-select a match-day squad of upto 15 players from the team squad. Prematch process should also have an auto select team option based on player performances. must have the ability to arrange players in the team.
- Wagon Wheel: A wagon wheel scoring tool with live match scoring, instant visualization of player shot patterns, interactive field interface, immediate data capture, color-coded indicators, dismissal and extras buttons.
- Scoring Zones: Scoring Zones Integration for granular analytics and strategic insights post-match, zone-based categorization, heatmap generation, comparative analytics to identify player tendencies, strengths, and weaknesses.
- Spider Chart: Visually represent the batting power, precision, and directionality based on the distance the ball travels in various directions around the field.
- School Profiles: School profiles with playing fields to determine fixtures and venues
- Live Match Scoring: Live Match Scoring refers to the real-time recording, tracking, and updating of scores and events occurring during a cricket match. It involves a detailed and systematic approach to capturing all match activities, from runs scored and wickets fallen, to extras, overs completed, and other key game events.

## Style Guidelines:

- Use green (#4CAF50) to represent the cricket field and a fresh start.
- Use white (#FFFFFF) for the background to provide clarity and contrast.
- Use blue (#2196F3) for interactive elements like buttons and links, providing a modern and engaging feel.
- Clean, sans-serif font for readability.
- Use clear, grid-based layouts to organize data effectively.
- Incorporate cricket-related icons for intuitive navigation and feature representation.
